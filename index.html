Direction

Building agents that remember, reason, and recover.

I am a Ph.D. student at George Mason University, advised by Prof. Xuan Wang. My current focus is on agent engineering: memory, tool-state reliability, long-context reasoning, and evaluation methods that make model behavior more inspectable and less error-prone.

I like projects where a clean modeling idea has to survive real experiments: long-context benchmarks, controlled ablations, GPU training workflows, local-first tools, and products that turn AI into usable learning or decision support experiences.

Research Expertise

Agent Engineering

Agent memory, tool-state tracking, provenance, error prevention, long-context retention, and evaluation for systems that need to act reliably over many steps.

Long-Sequence Modeling

State-space models, spectral sequence modeling, budgeted inference, Mamba-style ideas, and mechanisms for keeping useful information over long inputs.

Research Engineering

PyTorch/CUDA experiments, reproducible ablations, structured evaluation pipelines, local/API model judging, and remote GPU execution for research iteration.
